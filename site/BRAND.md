## Brand Identity

Frank & Louie’s is an Italian specialty goods shop in Rehoboth Beach, Delaware — the place families circle back to every summer, that neighbors hit every week, and that food lovers will drive out of their way to find. We bake the things you can’t get anywhere else — fresh breads, cookies, our own sauces, and the original buttercake people plan their week around — and we import the pantry staples that are hard to get right.
Our mother and grandmother’s love of baking originated in our family’s hometown of Campobello di Mazara, Sicily. A very strong family bond was shared through baking, creating truly magical moments in the kitchen. The Buttercake was created in the kitchen of our little specialty store right here in Rehoboth Beach, Delaware.


### Brand Pillars

- **Quality** — We make and package our own breads, cookies, sauces, and the original buttercake, and we only carry imports worth carrying. Quality isn’t a marketing line, it’s the price of putting the Frank & Louie’s name on the box. If it isn’t good enough for our own table, it doesn’t go on the shelf.

- **Authentic** — Mom’s real recipes, no shortcuts, real Italian specialty goods, and no buzzwords. We do the work so our customers never have to settle for the grocery-store version.

- **Family** — Family runs through everything we make and sell, and it’s why regulars, vacationers, and first-timers all get treated like one of our own.

- **Fun** — Warm, generous, and a little brash. We’re a retail and wholesale shop now, not a deli counter, but the personality is the same. We tease like family, brag about the food, and never take ourselves too seriously.


## Voice & Tone

Like the fun relative at the family table: warm, old-school, a little brash, and quick with a line — generous with food and with opinions.


**Always:**

- Playful, never mean. We’ll tease, the way family teases. The joke is always with the customer, never at them.

- Old-school, never stiff. Heritage and tradition, said plainly — no corporate gloss, no fake nostalgia.

- Quick and punchy. Short lines. Real words. We earn the smile fast and get out of the way of the food.

- Confident, a little brash. We stand behind our food. We’ll happily tell you the buttercake is the best thing you’ll eat all summer, because it is

- Generous and warm. We talk to people like family we’re glad to see. The welcome comes first.


**Never:**

- Mean or sarcastic at the customer’s expense. Teasing is affection, not attitude.

- Overly precious about food. Proud, yes. Snobby, never. Everyone’s welcome here.

- Stiff or formal. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it like you’re saying it out loud.

- Corporate or buzzwordy (“artisanal culinary experiences,” “elevated provisions”). We are real people.


**Good examples:**

- > Brag about the food. It’s allowed.

- > When in doubt, shorter and warmer.

- > Lead with the food, finish with the personality.

- > Say it like you’d say it across the kitchen table.
