# Frank & Louie's — Asset Shot List (for the scroll-cinema site)

The site is becoming an Apple-style scroll experience: products **float, scale, and
parallax** as you scroll. That look depends almost entirely on **clean cutouts**
(transparent PNGs) and a **consistent set** of photography.

> **The #1 rule:** cutouts must be cleanly masked — no white halo, no fuzzy fringe.
> A halo around a floating buttercake instantly reads "amateur." Shoot on clean,
> bright white, then mask to transparent.

> **Quality over quantity:** ~12–18 great, consistent assets beat 50 random ones.
> Shoot everything at the same eye-level/angle and white balance so they feel like
> one family.

---

## Technical specs (apply to all)

- **Cutouts:** PNG with transparency (24-bit + alpha). Deliver clean — **no baked-in
  background shadow** (I add soft shadows in CSS so they can float anywhere).
- **Resolution:** hero pieces **≥ 3000px** on the long edge; everything else
  **≥ 2200px**. Bigger is fine — I optimize for web.
- **Lighting:** soft, even, bright, warm and appetizing. Avoid harsh shadows.
- **Angle:** keep it consistent across the set — straight-on or a slight top-down,
  the same for every product.
- **Photos (non-cutout):** high-res JPEG. Provide a **horizontal** and, where you
  can, a **vertical** version (mobile crops to portrait).
- **Naming:** clear, e.g. `buttercake-vanilla-cut.png`, `brothers-cutout.png`.

---

## Tier 1 — must-have (the site leans on these)

1. **The hero buttercake — the money shot.** The cut-open one with the creamy
   center oozing, on transparent. Shoot 2–3: cut-open (hero), a whole one, and a
   little stack. Highest resolution of anything you shoot. *This is the single most
   important asset — it's the floating centerpiece that scales on scroll.*
2. **The two brothers — a clean cutout of Frank & Louie together.** Standing,
   aprons, a little brash/laughing. Ties straight to the two-faces logo and becomes
   the star of the story section. (A clean studio-ish background makes hair masking
   far easier.)
3. **One cutout per category (3 more):**
   - **Bread** — one gorgeous rustic loaf (and/or a focaccia). A couple of shapes
     reads as "always something different."
   - **Cookies** — a small **pile/cluster** of assorted cookies (not named ones —
     keeps it seasonal). A few crumbs are a bonus.
   - **Sauce** — the **jar(s)** on transparent. The trio together is great; single
     jars too.

## Tier 2 — makes it feel alive

4. **Ingredient accent cutouts** (small, for floating garnish + parallax depth):
   **fresh basil sprig, a tomato or two, garlic, a flour dusting / powdered-sugar
   puff.** These drift in the background and edges — the secret weapon for the
   "fun, alive, Italian" energy.
5. **"Process" / hands close-ups** (regular photos, not cutouts): kneading dough,
   filling a buttercake, dusting sugar, jarring sauce, pulling bread from the oven.
   These power the craft/old-world story and make great pinned/parallax moments.
6. **Mom (Diane)** — a warm portrait. Cutout if easy, otherwise a nice photo.

## Tier 3 — environment & atmosphere (full-bleed backgrounds)

7. **The storefront / The Brush Factory** — wide exterior, for a full-bleed
   "where to find us" moment.
8. **The bakery/kitchen in action** — warm, busy, a little steam.
9. **Rehoboth Beach vibe** — boardwalk / beach-town, to sell "the Rehoboth
   original."
10. **Atmosphere** — a busy counter, someone biting a buttercake, a happy customer.

A phone can work **if** you nail two things: **bright, even light** and a **clean
white background** (a sheet of white poster board + a window). The masking quality
is what matters, not the camera. Send me the cut-open buttercake and the two
brothers first — those two carry the most weight.
